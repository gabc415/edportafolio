About Me

Chemist and Chemistry Educator

Introduction

I am an experienced chemist and educator with a strong background in organic chemistry and a passion for integrating technology into teaching. I am currently completing my Ph.D. in Chemistry at the University of Nevada, Reno.

My research and teaching experience have provided me with a unique perspective on how technology can be used to enhance student learning and engagement in chemistry.

Teaching Experience

As a graduate instructor at San Francisco State University and a teaching assistant at the University of Nevada, Reno, I have had the opportunity to instruct undergraduate courses in chemistry labs and chemistry writing. My responsibilities included:

  • Developing and implementing engaging lesson plans
  • Providing mentorship and guidance to students
  • Assessing student progress through assignments and exams
  • Maintaining thorough documentation of student work

I am committed to creating a dynamic and inclusive learning environment where students can develop a strong foundation in chemistry and a passion for scientific inquiry.

Research Experience

My doctoral research has focused on exploring anionic Elbs and Friedel-Crafts reactions for the synthesis of novel 2D polymers. I have employed advanced techniques such as AFM, XRD, NMR, and STM to characterize the resulting structures, molecular architecture, surface morphology, and electronic properties.

I also have experience in applying machine learning algorithms for data analysis and materials modeling, contributing to improved compound synthesis and identification processes.

LMM Training and Prompt Engineering

I have working experience in leveraging my subject-matter expertise to train deep learning models, ensuring the accuracy of chemistry and science content. My previous responsibilities have included designing complex prompts for large language models, identifying biases, conducting rigorous testing, improving model reasoning, and evaluating outputs to enhance model performance.
