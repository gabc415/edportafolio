What I've Learned and How It's Changed My Thinking

This class has revealed much to me about the role of technology in education. Here are the key insights I have gathered:

1. Technology utilization with purpose, making it work for the needs of everyone.

I have come to understand that classroom technology must always serve a particular purpose. It is not a matter of simply using the latest technology but rather selecting tools that serve to enhance learning and assist students in reaching their objectives. I have come to appreciate the need to ensure that everyone has access to these tools. We must select technology that provides all students with various ways of learning and demonstrating knowledge.

2. Engaging Students and Involving Tools

I have explored many engaging tools that can make learning more interactive and enjoyable. Online simulations, group projects, and online games are tools that can really spark the interest of students, making learning an activity that they actually look forward to doing. Furthermore, I now also have a clearer vision of how I personally can utilize these tools in my own teaching, encouraging students to collaborate and exchange ideas.

3. New Ways to Keep Track of How Students Are Doing

I've witnessed the ability of technology to revolutionize student progress tracking, making it more interactive and authentic. I have discovered through my experiences how to use technology to make various forms of assessments, provide immediate feedback, and allow students to express their learning in numerous ways.

What's Next?

What I have learned in this class will alter the way I teach from now on. I hope to apply what was learned to enhance my classes, make them more interesting, and more accessible to all. I am excited about the continued exploration of new technologies and the development of my skills at integrating them into my teaching. Furthermore, this course has also enlightened me on how I can equip my students with the skills and know-how to successfully utilize technology and comprehend how it functions. This is much too significant nowadays, and I believe I am better prepared to enable them to do so.
